Kedarkantha Trek Highlights

  • Juda ka Talab

    Nestled in the lap of the majestic Himalayas, Juda ka Talab is a mysterious gem covered in tradition and mystery. Surrounded by snow-capped peaks and lush alpine meadows, Juda ka Talab attracts adventurers with its mysterious charm. The campsite near Juda ka Talab provides a peaceful retreat for tired hikers, where the sound of singing birds and rustling greenery soothes the soul. During the winter, Juda ka Talab changes into a magnificent beauty, buried in snow. Adventurers endure the cold to witness the frozen lake's otherworldly splendor, where each step feels like stepping into a winter fairytale. The journey to Juda ka Talab is a test of both physical endurance and spiritual fortitude, rewarding those who complete its difficult treks with lifelong memories.

  • Sunset

    If you are a person who likes to watch sunsets then this is heaven for you. People who have witnessed the sunset in Kedarkantha say it's the most magical thing ever experienced. In winter when the sunray hits the snow and the sky turns golden it is the most mesmerizing sight for nature lovers.

  • Wildlife Beauty

    The trek is more than simply a tour through stunning scenery; it is also an encounter with the vibrant species that inhabit this region. As you move along the twisting trails and rise to the snow-capped summits, you'll be immersed in a diverse tapestry of life. Keep your eyes out for the spectacular Himalayan Woodpecker, Himalayan Monal, Rosefinch, Musk Deer, Himalayan Black Bear, Himalayan Tahr, Himalayan Langurs, Leopards, squirrels, and the most common Himalayan Langur.

Overview - Kedarkantha Trek

As the name suggests, Kedarkantha translates to "Lord Shiva’s Throat." "Kedar" means ‘Shiva’ and "Kantha" means ‘Throat’. This trek is one of the most breathtaking treks located in Devbhoomi Uttarakhand. At a thrilling altitude of 12,500 feet above sea level, it provides one of the most panoramic views of the Himalayas, including ranges like Swargarohini, Bandarpoonch, Black Peak, Ranglana, Yamunotri, and Gangotri. Our Kedarkantha trek package will provide you with everything you need for the trek, with guidance.


Kedarkantha Trek Best Time to Visit

If you ask the Kedarkantha Trek best time to visit, then you should know that the Kedarkantha trek is open throughout the year for all adventurer trekkers. But if you want to witness snow-covered mountains, then the best time for you is during the winter in December and January. During the rainy season, it is recommended not to trek as it increases the risk. Trail Trekker has the best Kedarkantha trek package that is open for the entire year.

Kedarkantha Trek in Winter

If you want to discover the beautiful landscape covered in snow, then winter is the most preferred time for many trekkers. From December until February, you will be able to see this place completely covered in snow. Moreover, Juda ka Talab remains frozen until March starts.

Kedarkantha Trek in Summer

People who don’t like winter can visit during the summer from April to June. During summer, you can find some peace from the hot and humid weather. The overall temperature remains pleasant. But you will feel the sun rays hitting you directly, so don’t forget to carry your sunscreen.

Kedarkantha Trek in Autumn

If you don’t prefer winter or summer, then the ideal time for you is from September to November. Experience pleasant weather and a view from the top. Evenings get colder from October end, and you can witness some snow during November. The mountain is surrounded by lush green trees and flowers during the Autumn.


How to Reach Kedarkantha?

Are you planning to visit here any sooner? Then you can take our Kedarkantha trek package to begin your journey. Also, you should be aware of the routes and travel accommodations. First and foremost, you have to reach Dehradun. Here is how you can reach Dehradun from anywhere around India.

By Air

Many prefer to come to Dehradun via flight as it saves a lot of time but is costly too. You can book a direct flight to Dehradun Jolly Grant Airport. From the airport, you need to travel around 30 km to reach Dehradun. You can either book a cab or use local transportation like a bus.

By Road

You will find direct Volvo buses that will drop you at the Dehradun bus Station. However, it will take longer as compared to a flight but will save a lot of money.

By Train

If you want a more convenient means of travel, then the best option for you is the train. Nanda Devi, Kota Express, Shatabdi, Jan Shatabdi, and Vande Bharat are some of the best express trains to reach Dehradun.

Dehradun to Base Camp of Kedarkantha (Sankri)

Sankri is the base camp where the Kedarkantha trek begins. It is around 200 km from Dehradun to Sankri. Make sure to reach here prior to your trek day so you can rest well. You can reach Sankri from Dehradun by these 2 modes of transportation:

Buses

This is the cheapest traveling method. You can take a direct bus to Sankri from Dehradun. However, the travel time is around 8 hours. So, make sure you board a morning bus on time.

Cab

You can book a cab for yourself from Dehradun to Sankri. This will cost you extra as private cabs will charge you a lot for this distance.

You can choose transportation according to your convenience. Either way, you will enjoy a breathtaking view on the way to Kedarkantha.


Difficulty Level of Kedarkantha Trek

This beautiful trek is considered to be moderately difficult. The trek is good for both beginners and experienced trekkers. However, you still need to prepare yourself as it is located at a mind-blowing height of 12500 feet. You can face altitude challenges if you are a beginner and have never been to trek before. Proper preparation and physical fitness are important with appropriate gear to enhance your trekking experience.


Kedarkantha Trek Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival at Sankri.

Distance: 200 km

Duration: 9-10 hours

Altitude: 6500 ft/ 1980 m

The adventure begins in Dehradun at Prince Chowk near Railway station. The road trip is stunning from the start.

It begins with a journey through Mussoorie's magnificent highlands and the majestic Kempty Falls, which are nestled among steep mountain cliffs.

The roads are long and meandering, allowing you to take in all of the magnificent scenery. Swift mountain streams flow parallel to the road.

After reaching Mussoorie, the route goes beside a glittering Yamuna River for a bit. Eventually, the environment turns from deep gorges to an amazing canopy of blue pine forests lining either side of the road.

The final 22 kilometers to Sankri, however, steal the show as we go through the Govind National Park, encountering a variety of unusual vegetation and fauna.

The roads can be difficult and rocky in certain places, but the stunning vistas of the deep coniferous forests, tons of river valley, and scattered apple orchids more than compensate.

Sankri is a lush green village boasting some outstanding views of Mt. Swargarohini, especially during sunset time.

We will be staying in a guest house for the night and will begin our new journey to Juda-ka-Talab- the next morning.

Day 2: Sankri to Juda-ka-Talab.

Distance: 4 km

Duration: 4-5 hours

Altitude: 9100 ft/ 2770 m

The trek to the Kedarkantha summit begins in Sankri, our base village, and continues today to Juda Ka Talab, a magnificent alpine lake. Start your day early, have your breakfast and walk out directly towards Juda Ka Talab.

The lake is located at an elevation of 9100 feet, and the journey distance is approximately 4 kilometers. It will take about 4-5 hours to reach the lake.

As you begin your adventure out of Sankri village, you will venture into the village expansion Saur and from here you will walk along a river, and then we will take a turn into the forest of pine and maple, climbing strongly. We will cross many suspension bridges and small mountain villages.

Today's trial is comparatively broad and progressive. During the summer, the route is lush and green, and you'll see plenty of flowers along the way. The trail is covered in maple and pine leaves and leads to lush green meadows.

Kedar Kantha Trek Best Time to Visit is during the winter, the trail is snow-covered, and depending on the conditions, boots may be required to effectively travel the path.

As you hike uphill, you may see animals such as Himalayan Languors, Himalayan Woodpecker, Himalayan Monal, Rosefink, and Musk Deer. While they are extremely shy, they move fast, swinging from branch to branch, making it difficult to capture a proper view of them. Apart from this, you may see boars, hares, and martens.

We'll camp here for the night. You can take lovely shots here and go for nature walks in the surrounding region.

At night, a bonfire is an excellent place to enjoy your dinner and warm drinks while chatting with your fellow trekkers. The night sky is incredibly stunning, with an array of the brightest stars illuminating the sky. Sleep the night in your tents.

Day 3: Juda-ka-Talab to Kedarkantha Base Camp.

Distance: 4 km

Duration: 3-4 hours

Altitude: 11,000 ft/3350 m

We'll start the day with a warm breakfast and a beautiful sunrise view before beginning our climb to Base Camp. The base camp is 11,000 feet above sea level and 4 kilometers from Juda Ka Talab.

Walking moderately, you can get to the base camp in about three hours. The trail is slightly steeper than the previous day, and it steadily increases steeper as you approach the summit.

A short walk through brown oak forests covered with snow leads to a breathtaking snowfield surrounded by tall trees on one side and a great panorama of snow peaks on the other.

From Kedarkantha's Base Camp, you can spot Bandarpoonch, Swargarohini, and Kalanag from here.

On clear days, the Milky Way shines over the night sky, surrounded by a layer of white snow. Some areas of the Base Camp and peak are still covered in snow in early spring. By April, however, the snow has melted, revealing the vibrant colors of spring.

It's time to give your body some rest as we will be starting the next day's trek at around 2 am.

Day 4: Kedarkantha Base Camp to Kedarkantha Peak and Back to Kedarkantha Base Camp.

Distance: 11 km

Duration: 6-7 hours

Altitude: 12,500 ft/3800 m

Day 4 is the one you've been waiting for! The hike from the base camp to the summit is short, and once there, you'll see a breathtaking 360-degree view of the Uttarakhand peaks, the Chaainsheel Pass, and Himachal's Kailash range.

You may even see the Har Ki Dun valley below. The Kedarkantha top is marked by a group of stones with a Trishul pointing to the sky.

According to legend, the peak was intended to be the first Kedarnath temple, but Lord Shiva, disguised as a cow to avoid the Pandavas, was interrupted during meditation by the people. The interruption caused him to shift his position to the current Kedarnath shrine.

After spending some time we will come down to the base camp and spend our night there. The next day we will trek down to Sankri.

Day 5: Kedarkantha Base Camp to Sankri.

Distance: 6 km

Duration: 5-6 hours

Altitude: 6500 ft/2000 m

Today is our final day of trekking, and we will go down from Kedarkantha to Sankri, a 6-kilometer journey that will take us around 5-6 hours.

Once we get to Sankri, you can spend the rest of the day exploring the picturesque village and neighboring villages such as Gangad and Osla, which are noted for their friendly hospitality and traditional Pahari culture.

Sankri is also the final road heading into the Govind Balabh Pant Wildlife Sanctuary. Although there aren't many shops in Sankri, you may still purchase wooden handicrafts, toys, and home décor as memories of your trip.

Know about the local lifestyle and traditions in Sankri. Life in Sankri is slow-paced, and religious beliefs are strong.

Day 6: Departure from Sankri.

Distance: 200 km

Duration: 9-10 hours

Today is the last day of the Kedarkantha trek, our fantastic journey, and we will return to Dehradun with many wonderful memories.

Vehicles will be hired for you at Sankri to transport you back to Dehradun. Check out of the hotel in the morning so that we may start our travel to Dehradun early.

We have a distance of 200 kilometers to cover, which will take around 10 hours, so the earlier we start the better. We will return along NH 123, admiring the picturesque splendor.

Enjoy the nature around you, with breathtaking vistas stretching far and wide. We will arrive in Dehradun in the evening. Make necessary arrangements if you have to travel to other cities.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What’s Kedar Kantha Trek Best Time to Visit?

Kedarkantha Trek is known for its snow-covered mountains during the winter. If you want to see snow then December to February is best for you if not then you can visit anywhere between March to November.

2. Is the Kedarkantha Trek difficult?

Kedarkantha Trek is considered with moderate difficulty. Whether you are a beginner or expert you can easily complete this trek.

3. How long will we be traveling every day?

Our daily trekking time will typically be 5-6 hours, allowing ample time to savor the journey's every moment. However, the initial travel from Dehradun to Sankri will be around 9-10 hours.

4. Will there be a mobile network during the trek?

No, once you reach Sankri there won’t be any network. So, if you need to inform your family and friends it’s better to let them know before starting the trek. The only available network signal is BSNL.

5. Where does the Kedarkantha trek begin?

To begin the trek, you'll first come to Dehradun, and from there, you'll proceed to Sankri. Situated at an altitude of 6500 feet, Sankri serves as the starting point for the adventurous trek to Kedarkantha.
