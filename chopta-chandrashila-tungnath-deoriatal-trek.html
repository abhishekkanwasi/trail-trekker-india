Trek Highlights

  • Chandrashila Peak

    The Summit, also known as the "Moon Rock," is the peak of the Chandrashila Trek. The peak provides a breathtaking 360-degree panorama of the spectacular Himalayan peaks, which include Nanda Devi, Trishul, and Chaukhamba. Whether at sunrise or sunset, the view from Chandrashila is breathtaking.

  • Deoria Tal

    Deoria Tal, also known as 'Deoria', is a natural lake located about 3 kilometers up a hill from the villages of Mastura and Sari on the Ukhimath-Chopta road in Uttarakhand. The lake is located at a height of 7,800 feet/ 2,400 m in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand. Deoria Tal is bordered by lush woodlands and provides breathtaking views of snow-capped peaks in the background. The reflection of the towering Chaukhamba on the lake's crystal-pure waters is quite breathtaking.

  • Tungnath Temple

    The Tungnath trek is around 4 kilometers and located at a breathtaking height of 12,000 feet. The Tungnath Chandrashila Trek begins in the lovely town of Chopta. It leads trekkers to the Tungnath temple, the world's highest Shiva temple. This ancient sanctuary, set against a background of snow-capped peaks, raises spiritual energy and welcomes pilgrims and trekkers alike to enjoy its holy peace of mind.

  • Tungnath Kund

    On heading toward Tungnath, you will come across the magnificent Tungnath Kund. This sacred pond is surrounded by mythological tales, adding a mysterious element to the journey. It mirrors the neighboring peaks and offers a peaceful setting for meditation.

  • Scenic View of Landscape

    The path leads you through a range of surroundings, from lush meadows to deep woodlands that open up into panoramic views. These various landscapes provide a visual feast that changes with each step.

Chopta Tungnath Trek Overview

The Chopta Tungnath Trek offers an incredible view during our hikes. The summit offers some of the best landscapes in the Indian Himalayas. It's like a huge feast there's so much to take in that you can't get it all in. Many trekkers will never forget this trek.

The Gangotri peaks, Mt Thalay Sagar, Kedarnath, and the Kedar Dome can all be seen from the top. Mandani Parbat and Janhukut. The majestic Chaukhamba stands straight in front. Most of them stand above 7,000 meters tall.

Gauri Parbat, Hathi Parbat, and Dunagiri are all on your right side. Then directly ahead, standing against the horizon on India's tallest mountain, Mt Nanda Devi. Trekkers find it difficult to believe they are seeing Mount Nanda Devi.

In the spring, the trek is particularly memorable. The trail that goes through the canopy of oak and rhododendron trees is almost like a dream. This trek is picture-perfect, and you'll pass through one of the best rhododendron forests, which light up the mountains in March and April.

The Deoriatal Chopta Tungnath Trek runs through picturesque forests and provides a breathtaking view of the snow-capped peaks. The walk provides an opportunity to see the 1000-year-old Tungnath temple, Lord Shiva's tallest shrine. It is one of the Panch-Kedars, which are thought to have been built by Arjuna.

Chandrashila Peak is well-known for its stunning panoramic views of the Himalayan peaks. It is one of the few peaks that can be climbed during the winter season. Our Chopta Chandrashila with Tungnath & Deoriatal Trek package, you can have a seamless travel experience.


Chopta Chandrashila with Tungnath & Deoriatal Trek Best Time

Even though Chopta Chandrashila or Deoriatal Chandrashila is a high-altitude trek, it is accessible for nearly 8-9 months of the year. The trek is accessible for ten months of the year, except during the rainy season which is from July to mid-September.

If we remove the monsoons, we have four separate seasons to enjoy Deoriatal Chandrashila: winter, spring, summer, and autumn. Each season is unique and beautiful for Chopta Chandrashaila with Tungnath & Deoriatal Trek Best Time.

Chopta Tungnath Trek in Spring

The temperature hovers around 15°C during the day and drops to 0°C at night. Spring in Deoriatal Chandrashila begins in early March and continues until the end of April. The snow melts in the upper reaches, while the grass in the lower meadows begins to turn green. Small flowers grow from the grass, while rhododendrons blossom on trees. The top portions of the journey are generally covered in snow until mid-April.

Chopta Chandrashaila with Tungnath & Deoriatal Trek best time in spring is an excellent season to see color differences in the meadows. You see snow, grass, and the color of flowers all combined.

Chopta Tungnath Trek in Summer

The temperature during the day hovers around 17 to 25°C. At night, the temperature can drop below 10°C. Summer brings out a green color palette on the path you may not see in any other season. The trees and meadows appear bright and sunny from May to June. Early in June, slopes may receive minimal summer showers, and the vegetation becomes flourishing. The trek is at its easiest and provides an enjoyable break from the summer heat of the plains.

Chopta Tungnath Trek in Autumn

Chopta Chandrashila with Tungnath & Deoriatal Trek best time is in the months following the monsoon season: September, October, and November. The sky has been cleared of haze following the monsoon season. Autumn provides breathtaking views of the Garhwal Himalayas. The temperature will be around 10-15°C in the morning and drops below 5°C at night, and can even drop to 0°C.

Chopta Tungnath Trek in Winter

In winter, people trek to Deoriatal Chandrashila to enjoy the snow and breathtaking views. When the landscape is covered in white snow, the colors shift again. Soft, powdery snowflakes hang freely from pine trees, lending a Christmas-like air. It's ideal for snowball battles and snowfall if you're lucky. However, snow makes the trek more challenging. So, if you're going in winter, make sure you prepare properly. During winter, the temperature reaches -10°C.


How to Reach Rishikesh

Are you planning to visit here any sooner than you can take our Chopta Chandrashila with Tungnath & Deoriatal Trek package to begin your journey? Also, you should be aware of the routes and travel accommodations. First and foremost you have to reach. Here is how you can reach Rishikesh from anywhere around India.

By Air

Many prefer to come to Rishikesh via flight as it saves a lot of time but is costly too. You can book a direct flight to Rishikesh Jolly Grant Airport. From the airport, you need to travel around 15 km to reach Rishikesh. Book a cab or use local transportation to reach the Rishikesh bus stand from the airport.

By Road

You will find direct Volvo buses that will drop you at the Rishikesh bus Station. It takes longer hours compared to flights but will save a lot of money.

By Train

If you want a more convenient means of travel then the best option for you is the train. Nanda Devi, Kota Express, Shatabdi, Jan Shatabdi and Vande Bharat are some of the best express trains to reach Rishikesh.

How to Reach Chopta Trek From Rishikesh

If you are planning to go for the Chopta Tungnath Trek alone or with your friends then you can come here with 2 different modes of transportation.

Buses

This is the cheapest traveling method. You can take a direct bus to Chopta from Rishikesh. However, the travel time is around 8 hours. So, make sure you board a morning bus on time.

Cab

You can book a cab from Rishikesh to Chopta. This will cost you extra as private cabs will charge you a lot for this distance.

These are the best modes of transportation for solo travelers. Either way, you will be enjoying the natural scenic view of this beautiful place.


Chopta Tungnath Trek Difficulty

The Deoriatal-Chandrashila Trek is easy-moderate, climbing up to 12,000 feet. Deoriatal-Chandrashila is a safe trek. The elevation of 12,000 feet is covered over 3 to 4 days, giving your body enough time to recover. Even if the trek is easy to moderate in difficulty, it is still vital to be aware of potential mistakes.


Chopta Chandrashila with Tungnath & Deoriatal Trek Itinerary

Day 1: Reach Chopta.

Distance: 200 km

Altitude: 8,500 feet/ 2600 m

Duration: 7-8 hours

Our day will start early. The pickup location will be from the Rishikesh bus stand, railway station, and Natraj Chowk. Enjoy the peaceful beauty of the mountains on the way to Chopta. The ride may be tiring because it is a Himalayan drive, but the breathtaking views will keep you refreshed. When you arrive, check into the camp/homestay, and spend the rest of the evening at leisure.

Day 2: In Chopta (Trek to Tungnath & Chandrashila) by Drive to Sari Village.

Distance: 6 km trek from Chopta to Tungnath and Chandrashila, Chopta to Sari Village 65 km

Altitude: Tungnath: 12,000 feet/ 3600 m, Chandrashila: 12,100 feet/ 3700 m

Duration: 2-3 hours trek and 2-3 hours drive

On the 2nd day of your journey, we will leave Chopta/Sari Village and head to Tungnath, Chandrashila. The trek will consist of going and returning, with a well-defined stony trail providing a clear view of the Chandrashila mountain and the brilliant colors of Tungnath temple.

A few hours into your hike, you will encounter the first obstacle: walking on the steep upward bend on the mountain's ridgeback. The trail will be covered in Rhododendron blossoms throughout. When you get to the Tungnath temple, take time to worship Lord Shiva.

Tungnath is among the highest Lord Shiva temples in the world. After the temple visit, we will trek to Chandrashila peak. Spend some time at the peak and watch the beautiful panoramic view of some of the highest peaks. Later we'll be coming back to Sari village, eating dinner and spending the night in our hotel.

Day 3: Departure from Sari.

Distance: 200 km

Altitude: 6,600 feet/ 2000 m

Duration: 7-8 hours

This is our last day of the trek. Get up early and pack your stuff as we will be checking out from our hotel. We will be leaving for Rishikesh early in the morning. On the way to Rishikesh, we will stop for a lunch break and further continue our journey. Enjoy every moment as we will return to Rishikesh by evening. If you have to go further make your necessary arrangements as Chopta Chandrashila with Tungnath & Deoriatal Trek package will end once you reach Rishikesh.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. How long is Chopta Tungnath Trek?

The distance from Chopta to Tungnath is 3 km. To reach Chandrashila peak, you must trek around 2 more km after reaching Tungnath.

2. Will I find snow in Tungnath in November?

In November, expect some snow when trekking up the hills, but the best viewing for fresh snow would be ideal from December through February.

3. How difficult is the Chopta Tungnath trek?

The Chopta trek is considered moderately difficult and typically takes 3 to 4 days to complete. Whether you are a new trekker or have experience, this trip is suitable for you.

4. How do you get to Chopta Trek?

You can reach Chopta by either taking a bus from Rishikesh or Haridwar or booking a private cab.

5. Is it allowed to stay in Tungnath or Chandrashila?

No, you cannot stay at Tungnath or Chandrashila as there are no accommodation facilities available. Additionally, camping is not permitted in these areas.
