  • Trekking

    Adventurers and nature lovers, this place is heaven for you. This trek is a walk between natural beauty where you will see a waterfall, lush green trees, rivers, and more. This is an excellent opportunity for trekkers in the Valley to explore various sections of the area and see lovely flowers and natural hills. Trek to the Valley of Flowers, a magnificent meadow not to be missed.

  • Hemkund Sahib

    The highest Gurudwara in the world Hemkund Sahib is a must-visit. The gurudwara is located at a mind-blowing height of 15,000 feet Hemkund Sahib is one of Uttarakhand's most revered Sikh shrines, with hundreds of pilgrims making the hard journey every year. Throughout the winter, the temple sleeps beneath a thick blanket of snow. As a result, devotees have a fantastic opportunity to receive Guru Ji's blessings. This is the perfect time for you to visit this amazing place.

  • Pandukeshwar Temple

    Pandukeshwar, nestled at 1900 meters above sea level, is a sacred site en route to Badrinath, just 18 kilometers away from Joshimath. Legend has it that this was where King Pandu, the father of the legendary Pandavas from the Mahabharata, worshiped Lord Shiva. The area boasts two renowned temples: the Yog Dhyan Badri Temple, which is part of the "Sapta Badri" or seven of Badri, and the Temple of Lord Vasudev. During the winter months when Badrinath closes, Yog Dhyan Badri Temple serves as the home for Utsava-murti. It is believed that the Pandavas themselves constructed the Temple of Lord Vasudev here.

Overview - Valley Of Flowers Trek

If you truly are a nature lover and want to explore an ultimate destination then the Valley of Flowers Trek is for you. The Valley of Flowers Trek is a renowned and lovely trekking destination in the Garhwal Himalayas. It provides magnificent views of vibrant flowers, lush green fields, and the snow-capped Himalayas. Situated at a breathtaking height of 12,000 feet it is declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO.

In 1931, a group of mountaineers returning from a trip to Mount Kamet stumbled into the Valley of Flowers by chance. Frank Smythe, Eric Shipton, and RL Holdsworth were members of this team. It was declared a national park in 1980, and it became the second core zone of the Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve in 2000.

Valley of Flowers Trek best time to visit is between July to September. The place is approximately 2 km broad and 8 km long. The temperature on this journey often ranges between 5 and 20 degrees Celsius, and you may expect moderate rains even with a dense cloud cover for the majority of the day.

This place is home to many rare flora and fauna that will add to your beautiful trek journey. The place has an astonishing 13 animal species, each contributing to the region's diverse fauna. The Valley of Flowers is a biodiverse haven, with up to 520 plant kinds. This hike showcases the Himalayan region's remarkable biodiversity.


Valley of Flowers Trek Best Time

Are you passionate about the trek and want to visit this mesmerizing place? You may want to know what the Valley of Flowers Trek best time is. This is one of the fewer treks that can be done during the monsoon. You will experience the valley in full bloom and breathtaking view. Monsoon showers enhance this place more with vibrant flowers. So if you want to plan your trip to the Valley of Flowers trek then visit between June to September. Plan your next trip with our Valley of Flowers Trek package and enjoy hassle-free travel.

Valley of Flowers Trek in June

The Valley of Flowers' gates open to trekkers in June. During this month, the daisy-fresh breeze of the Himalayas attracts nature enthusiasts and travelers looking for peace and relaxation. The temperature is around 16C. In this month, flowers in the Valley begin to bloom, with tiny petals and short stems.

Valley of Flowers Trek in July

During July, the Valley turns green. Because of its high altitude, the valley will have some snow patches on the trail until the middle of July. In July, the Valley of Flowers' temperature ranges between 19 and 20 degrees Celsius. Snowdrifts can be found on the hike trails leading up to Hemkund. It enhances the beauty of the scenery. Later in July, monsoon showers intensify, completely removing the snow. The flowers in the Valley begin to blossom gradually.

Valley of Flowers Trek in August

In August, this place becomes a real-life paradise. The weather is cool and rainy, perfect for flowers to bloom. Everywhere you look, colorful flowers paint the landscape, making it incredibly beautiful. At the start of the month, green patches decorate the valley, but by the end, it's like walking through a golden sea of flowers. It's a magical sight that you have to see to believe!

Valley of Flowers Trek in September

This month, the Valley is covered in glossy pink and crimson red flowers, making it look stunning. The green meadows are filled with lush vegetation. The weather is nice with clear skies, offering great views of the mountains. Days are around 23°C, but it can get a bit cooler in the evenings.

This is also the time when many pilgrims and devotees visit Hemkund Sahib. It's known for its beautiful atmosphere, holy Gurudwara, temple, and crystal-clear lake. You'll also find plenty of Brahma Kamal flowers there, adding to the beauty of the place.


How to Reach Valley of Flowers Trek

With our Valley of Flowers trek package you will get all the benefits at a very affordable price. If you are planning to visit this beautiful place then you should know how you can reach the destination. Valley of Flowers is located in Uttarakhand, and to reach here, you need to come to Dehradun.

By Road

You can take a direct bus or cab to Dehradun. However, it will take longer to reach via road but it is affordable.

By Train

If you want a convenient journey then the best option for you is the train. Nanda Devi, Kota Express, Shatabdi, Jan Shatabdi and Vande Bharat are some of the best express trains to reach Dehradun.

By Air

Take a direct flight to Jolly Grant Airport. It will save a lot of time but will cost you more. You need to travel 30 km more from the airport to reach Dehradun.


Difficulty Level of Valley of Flowers Trek

This breathtaking trek is considered to be moderately difficult. So, if you are a beginner or expert you can easily complete this trek. Valley of Flower is situated at a mind-blowing height of 12,000 feet therefore you need to prepare yourself well. Proper preparation and physical fitness is a must with appropriate gear that will make your journey even better. Some people may face altitude challenges so better prepare yourself for it.


Valley of Flowers Trek Itinerary

Day 1: Reach Joshimath.

Distance: 247 km

Altitude: 2000 ft/ 610 m to 6200 ft./ 1890 m

Duration: 8-9 hours

Our beautiful journey will begin in the morning from the heart of Uttarakhand in Dehradun. The distance we will be covering is around 300 km from Rishikesh to Joshimath. It will take 8-9 hours to reach our destination. The trip is long but it will be worth it as you will be witnessing some picturesque views throughout the way.

On our journey we will be crossing 4 holy rivers Devprayag, Rudraprayag, Karnaprayag, and Nandprayag, providing plenty of photographic opportunities and life-changing experiences. Thus, be alert and prepare your cameras to record the amazing beauty of this heavenly journey.

Day 2: Joshimath to Pulna by drive and trek to Ghangaria.

Altitude: 6,200 ft./ 1890 m to 9,500 ft./ 2900 m

Distance: Joshimath to Pulna drive 6km. The Trek distance is 10 km

Duration: 5-6 hours

The morning will start with a healthy breakfast, leaving our rooms, and a 15-minute drive to Pulna.

We begin our walk from Pulna towards Ghangaria. The distance we need to cover today is 10 kilometers, which will take approximately 5-6 hours.

The trail leading to Ghangaria is well-marked, making the journey easy. However, constant ascent can become exhausting.

The walking road to Ghangaria is filled with little dhabas, pilgrims heading to Hemkund Sahib, and an aggressive flow of the Pushpavati River. You will also be surrounded by trees during the trip, which makes the journey more pleasant.

Day 3: Ghangaria - Valley of Flowers Trek and back to Ghangaria.

Altitude: 9,500 ft./ 2900 m to 12,000 ft./ 3600 m

Distance: 4 km

Duration: 5-6 hours

Today is the day you were eagerly waiting for. We rise early, eat breakfast and begin our journey. We will start from Ghangaria to the Valley of Flowers.

You will undoubtedly have many unforgettable moments from this day, including breathtaking views of dreamland. Because this valley is both a true beauty and a natural paradise, the journey there will let you feel the comfort of nature without the bustle of the city.

The trek is around 4 km from Ghangaria. Here, the Pushpawati River flows beneath the wooden bridge, providing a breathtaking display of nature's beauty. The valley will provide you with breathtaking views and experiences that will surpass anything you've ever imagined. The Himalayan peak views are magnificent. At the Valley of Flowers journey, you can also discover several local flowers such as dog flowers, blue poppy, hooked stick seed, white leaf, meadow geranium, hog foot, and Himalayan rose.

Witness a panoramic view of the Himalayan peaks, including Rattaban, Bhyundar Khal, Gauri Parvat, and Nilgiri Parvat. We will head back to Ghangaria before evening and end our day with a delicious meal.

Day 4: Ghangaria - Hemkund Sahib Trek and back to Ghangaria.

Altitude: 9,500 ft./ 2,900 m to 15,000 ft./ 4250 m

Distance: 12 Km

Duration: 6-7 hours

On the fourth day, We will wake up early, eat breakfast and begin our hike to Hemkud Sahib, which is around 6 kilometers away. Hemkund Sahib is one of the most praised Sikh sanctuaries; it is the world's highest Gurudwara, at an elevation of 15,000 feet.

While trekking from Ghangaria to Hemkund Sahib, you will come across multiple tea vendors and a Dhaba. Brahma Kamal blooms widely along this route. With a modest incline, the climb is continuous. It will take us approximately 3 to 4 hours to reach Hemkund Sahib.

The lake is a sight to behold, especially during the monsoon season when it remains hazy. We will spend some time witnessing the beauty. This area is nestled between snow-capped mountains and a gorgeous peaceful lake, providing a breathtaking perspective of the mystical surroundings.

Clear skies allow you to see the perfect view of the butting Saptrishi summits. This lake is mostly maintained by dissolving ice masses from Saptrishi peaks and Hathi Parvat. In addition, a strict Laxman sanctuary is located around the lake.

The environment surrounding the lake is so fascinating that it will undoubtedly blow your mind with its stunning beauty and fascinating panoramas of gorgeous flowers such as Brahmakamal, Blue Poppy, and Vajradanti.

As the weather can turn harsh at any time we will be heading back to Ghangaria by the evening. The day will end with hot and delicious food.

Day 5: Ghangaria - Pulna Trek 10 km and drive to Joshimath.

Altitude: 9,500 ft./ 2900 m to 6,200 ft./ 1890 m

Distance: 10 km trek from Ghangaria to Pulna and 6 km drive to Joshimath

Duration: 5-6 hours

Today is our last day of the trek. We will trek down from Ghangaria to Pulna. The distance we will be covering is 10 km. and it will take 5-6 hours. Once we reach Pulna we will head back to Joshimath. We spend our night in the hotel so you can get some good rest. You can recall all the memories of this trek and the quality time you spent here. Valley of Flowers will be the ultimate trek for you with breathtaking views with a walk in nature. Get some good sleep as we will leave in the next morning.

Day 6: Departure from Joshimath.

Distance: 300 km

Duration: 8-9 hours

Our beautiful journey comes to an end today. We will be having breakfast in the morning and will check out from our rooms. We will head back to Rishikesh. The travel journey is 300 km and it will take around 8-9 hours to reach. We will be reaching Rishikesh by evening or night so if you have to travel further make prior arrangements.


Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What’s Valley of Flowers Trek Best Time?

If you want to visit the Valley of Flowers trek then you should consider anywhere between June to September. During this time the valley is covered in lush green and vibrant flowers.

2. Is it difficult to trek in the Valley of Flowers Trek?

The valley of flowers is considered as easy to moderate in terms of difficulty. So, if you are a beginner or expert trekker you can easily complete this trek. But you need to be physically fit to enjoy the trek.

3. How long does it take to reach this place from Dehradun?

The distance from Dehradun to the Valley of Flowers is around 300 km. and it takes around 8-10 hours to reach there.

4. How many days does it take to finish this hike?

It will take around 6-7 days to complete the trek. It will include your travel time to reach the location, staying on camping sites, and returning to Dehradun.

5. Does it snow in the Valley of Flowers Trek?

Yes, snowfall begins from December until winter ends. As the Valley of Flowers is situated at an astonishing height of 12,000 feet the valley is covered in snow and makes it difficult to reach there as the trek remains closed in winter.
