Overview – Dayara Bugyal Trek

The Dayara Bugyal Trek is a panoramic view of a high-altitude alpine meadow trek in Gangotri National Park, nestled in the Uttarkashi district of Uttarakhand. At an altitude of 3,750 meters, experience vast fields full of grass in spring or thick sheets of snow in winter. Dayara Bugyal beckons both novice hikers and seasoned trekkers alike. In winter, it transforms into a canvas of adventure, attracting large groups who chase the skies on their skis. The trek is an easy trek difficulty that covers a distance of 24km through different landscapes but the whole trail will be attached to the views of the mighty Himalayas Mountains always like Mt Bandarpoonch and Mt Srikanth. The best time to visit Dayara Bugyal is from May to November when the meadow is lush and the weather is favorable for trekking.

Dayara Bugyal Nature's Symphony

Dayara Bugyal is an ensemble of nature’s wonders, where each step takes you closer to the heart of the Himalayas. In spring and summer, the meadows are covered with a riot of wildflowers, painting the landscape green, yellow, pink, and purple. The fresh mountain air and the birdsong are so soothing it rejuvenates the soul. As you walk the trail, the oak and rhododendron forests open up to reveal the meadows and the snow-capped peaks of Bandarpoonch, Srikanth, and Gangotri in the background. Each season is different and equally beautiful, making Dayara Bugyal a year-round destination for nature lovers.

Mythological Significance: Legends of the Sacred Land

The area around Dayara Bugyal is steeped in mythology, the region is full of stories and legends. According to local tales, these meadows were the playground of the gods. The word “Bugyal” means “high altitude meadow” in the Garhwali language, and it is believed that these meadows were the favorite of the gods because of their beauty and peace. The nearby village Barsu, the starting point of the trek, has its history and culture, the traditions, and practices are passed down from generation to generation. Trekkers often get lost in these stories and it adds a spiritual dimension to their journey.

Dayara Bugyal - Cultural Significance

Barsu, the village from which you start trekking to Dayara Bugyal, is indeed culturally rich. Over centuries, the local Garhwali people have retained their customs and traditions. Legends of gods and heroes infuse the local folklore, which is resplendent with age-old rituals and ceremonies still performed by tribes settled in this belt. The villagers have a redoubtable, abiding bond with nature as they work together to sustain the land. Traditional practices such as livestock rearing, agricultural farms, and weaving support the primary economic activities of the local people. During this cultural setting, you also manage to find yourself getting an idea about how life, in general, is up there amongst these simple yet incredible mountain people living their lives.

Butter Festival 2024 (Anduri Utsav)

The place is very famous because of some unique and vibrant cultural events, like the Dayara Bugyal Butter Festival or Anduri Utsav, held to welcome tourists. The festivals bloom in the hearts of villagers such as Raithal, Barsu, and many other surrounding settlements.

Origin and Meaning: Spread across 3 days, The Butter Festivity indicates the celebration of the end of the monsoon & the starting up of harvesting. It is normally a thanksgiving festivity to the village deities, for an abundant harvest and the well-being of their livestock. The festival is named after the inclusion of butter in different rituals and playful activities.

Celebrations and Activities: During the festival, villagers dress in traditional attire and gather in Dayara Bugyal to partake in a series of rituals and festivities. One of the main attractions is the playful butter smearing, where participants apply butter to each other’s faces and bodies, symbolizing prosperity and the richness of the harvest. Traditional Garhwali music and dance performances add to the festive atmosphere, with locals singing folk songs and performing the "Pandav Nritya" dance. The festival also features traditional sports and games, making it a lively and joyous occasion. The Butter Festival not only strengthens community bonds but also attracts visitors and trekkers who get to experience the vibrant culture and traditions of the region firsthand.


Dayara Bugyal Trek Best Time

Spring (March to April)
  • Day Temperature: 10°C to 15°C
  • Night Temperature: 0°C to 5°C

Spring breathes new life into the Dayara Bugyal Trek. The alpine meadows start to come alive with fresh greenery and blooming wildflowers, offering a stunning contrast against the lingering snow at higher altitudes. The days are comfortably cool, while nights can be quite chilly. This season is ideal for those who enjoy trekking in pleasant weather and witnessing the rejuvenation of nature along the trek route through oak forests and rhododendron forests.

Summer (May to June)
  • Day Temperature: 15°C to 20°C
  • Night Temperature: 5°C to 10°C

Summer is the Dayara Bugyal Trek's peak trekking season. The meadows are in full bloom, with vibrant flowers and lush grass creating a picturesque landscape. The days are warm and pleasant, perfect for long hikes and enjoying the panoramic mountain views of the Himalayan peaks in the Garhwal Himalayas. Nights are cool, making for comfortable camping experiences at Gui campsite and Chilapada. This season provides the best conditions for a beginner-friendly trek for families to experience the scenic beauty of Uttarakhand.

Monsoon (July to September)
  • Day Temperature: 12°C to 18°C
  • Night Temperature: 5°C to 10°C

The monsoon season brings heavy rainfall, making the Dayara Bugyal trek challenging due to slippery trails and potential landslides. However, for those who enjoy the lush greenery and the sound of rain, it offers a unique experience. The meadows are at their greenest, and the entire Uttarkashi district feels fresh and revitalized. Trekkers must be prepared for wet conditions and possible disruptions in their Dayara Bugyal itinerary.

Autumn (October to November)
  • Day Temperature: 10°C to 15°C
  • Night Temperature: -1°C to 5°C

Autumn is another excellent time for the Dayara Bugyal Trek. The monsoon recedes, leaving behind clear skies and crisp air. The meadows retain their greenery, and the changing colors of the trees add a beautiful contrast to the landscape. Days are cool and pleasant, while nights get colder at the high altitude of Dayara Bugyal. This season is perfect for trekkers looking for clear views of the Gangotri range and fewer crowds on the trek route from Raithal village to Dayara Top.

Winter (December to February)
  • Day Temperature: 0°C to 8°C
  • Night Temperature: -5°C to -10°C

Winter transforms the Dayara Bugyal trek route into a snow-covered wonderland, ideal for a winter trek and skiing. The entire region is blanketed in snow, offering a different kind of beauty and adventure. Days are cold, and nights can be freezing, making it suitable only for experienced trekkers and adventure enthusiasts. The winter landscape is serene and offers a unique trekking experience to witness Dayara Bugyal winter trek.

Dayara Bugyal Trek Best Time to Explore

The Dayara Bugyal trek's best time to visit is during the summer months (May to June) for a summer trek and autumn (October to November). These seasons offer the most favorable trekking conditions, with pleasant weather, clear skies, and breathtaking views. Summer is perfect for experiencing Dayara Bugyal in summer with the full bloom of the meadows, while autumn provides crisp air and clear vistas of the surrounding peaks like Mt Bandarpoonch.

How to Reach Dehradun the Pickup Point for Dayara Bugyal Trek

To begin the Dayara Bugyal trek, you first need to reach Dehradun. Here’s how you can make your way there:

By Flight
  • The closest airport is Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun Uttarakhand. It has good connections to big cities in India.
  • Once you land, you can get a taxi or hop on a bus to Dehradun city. This is where you start your drive to Raithal.
By Train
  • Dehradun Railway Station is a key train stop. Trains come here from Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, and other places.
  • When you get off the train, you can use local transport to get where you need to go.
By Road
  • Dehradun has good road links. You can hop on a bus or drive from Delhi (about 240 km).
  • Uttarakhand Roadways and private companies often run buses from Delhi ISBT Kashmere Gate.

When you get to Dehradun, hikers meet up at a spot they've agreed on before. From there, they get rides to Raithal or Barsu where the hike starts. Dayara Bugyal Trek Difficulty Level.

The Dayara Bugyal trek is considered to be an easy to moderate-level trek, making it suitable for both novice and experienced trekkers. It involves gentle ascents and descents, providing a balanced trekking experience.

Physical Requirements
  • Fitness Level: Basic to Moderate
  • Trekkers should have a reasonable level of fitness, with the ability to walk for several hours a day. Regular exercise or prior trekking experience is beneficial but not mandatory.
Altitude Considerations
  • Maximum Altitude: 3,750 meters (Dayara Bugyal Top)
  • The trek reaches a significant altitude, but the gradual ascent helps with acclimatization. Trekkers should be aware of basic altitude sickness symptoms and take necessary precautions.
Terrain and Trail Conditions
  • Varied Terrain: The trail includes forest paths, meadow crossings, and rocky patches. Good trekking shoes and gear are essential for comfort and safety.
  • Seasonal Variations: The difficulty can slightly increase during winter due to snow-covered trails, requiring additional preparation and gear.

Our Dayara Bugyal trek package is an excellent choice for trekkers seeking a balanced and rewarding Himalayan experience. Its easy-to-moderate difficulty level makes it accessible to beginners while still offering enough challenge and breathtaking views to satisfy seasoned trekkers.


Dayara Bugyal Trek Itinerary

Day 1: Dehradun to Raithal Village (182 km Drive).

Distance: Approximately 180 km by road

Time Taken: 7-8 hours

Altitude: Dehradun (640 m) to Raithal (1,800 m)

Difficulty: Easy

Weather Conditions: The weather in Raithal is generally cool, with temperatures ranging from 10°C to 20°C. The Dayara Bugyal temperature varies depending on the season.

Day 1st starts as you drive around the picturesque journey of a mere 182 km from Dehradun to Raithal Village. The scenic 6-7 hours drive through fertile valleys and quaint villages built on terraced fields along the crystal-clear water from Himalayan sources makes you comfortable at any altitude. The road journey will take you through the town's Rokeline (Uttarkashi) from the start and Rokline to the endpoint, via Raithal Village. On the way, enjoy the beauty of nature with your family. Perched at an altitude of 7,000 ft., this trek starts from the Raithal village. This is where you will spend the night in a homely guesthouse, soaking up Garhwali culture and warmth, gearing up for what lies ahead.

Day 2: Raithal to Gui Trek

Distance: 5 km trek.

Time Taken: 4-5 hours

Altitude: Raithal (1,800 m) to Gui (2,900 m)

Difficulty: Easy to Moderate

Weather Conditions: Temperatures range from 5°C to 15°C. Evenings can be cold, so warm clothing is necessary. The Dayara Bugyal weather can vary during the trek.

Day 2nd involves a 6 km trek from Raithal to Gui through rhododendron and oak forests, with the possibility of watching some wildlife. This trek offers excellent views in all directions. The trek is graded as easy, perfect for both beginners and experienced trekkers. The climb is gradual, and the views of Himalayan peaks keep getting better. In the afternoon, you will reach the Gui campsite, where you can relax and explore the area with a picturesque view of white peaks lying on one side in this serene environment.

Day 3: Reach Dayara Bugyal Top and Return to Gui.

Distance: 7 km trek (round trip)

Time Taken: 6-7 hours

Altitude: Gui (2,900 m) to Dayara Bugyal (3,750 m)

Difficulty: Moderate

Weather Conditions: Temperatures range from -2°C to 12°C. The weather can be unpredictable, with possible snow in winter, making it a great winter trek option. Dayara Bugyal in summer offers lush green meadows.

Day 3rd is the climax of your trek. From Gui to Dayara Bugyal top is an approximate 5 km round trip. This section offers sprawling meadows and views of major Himalayan peaks such as Bandarpoonch and Tirsuli. The Gui to Dayara Bugyal top trek is a visual delight with lush meadows that look different in summer and are covered under fresh snow during the winter months. After climbing, enjoy panoramic views and capture wonderful images. A leisurely walk amidst the huge meadows before heading back to Gui. That night at the Gui campsite, enjoy a warm meal and share stories around an open fire.

Day 4: Back to Raithal and Dehradun Departure.

Distance: 5 km trek and 180 km by road

Time Taken: 3-4 hours trek and 7-8 hours drive

Altitude: Gui (2,900 m) to Raithal (1,800 m) to Dehradun (640 m)

Difficulty: Easy

Weather Conditions: The weather will be similar to the first day, with cooler temperatures at higher altitudes.

On the last day, trek from Gui campsite to Raithal, a 5 km distance covered in around 3-4 hours. The hike from Gui camp to Raithal is a nice downhill walk offering one last chance to experience this trail. From Raithal, head back to Dehradun on the same day, concluding your epic Dayara Bugyal trek. This final portion provides time to reflect on the beautiful landscapes and experiences of your adventure.


Dayara Bugyal Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is the Dayara Bugyal trek famous for?

The Dayara Bugyal trek stands out with its magnificent features. It is especially famous for huge meadows of alpine grass that seem to stretch into far distances. The trekkers see panoramic views of the majestic Himalayan peaks. It really makes for a view to be remembered. Finally, there is the cultural treat with the Butter Festival experience, which adds a singular charm to the trek.

2. Best time to visit Dayara Bugyal

The best time to visit Dayara Bugyal depends on your choice of experience. If you want to see lush green meadows, trekkers get these from May to November, and at this time, wildflowers punctuate the green landscape. If you want a snow-bound Wonderland, come from December to February to find the terrain turned into a winter wonderland.

3. What is the Dayara Bugyal Trek route, the trek distance, and the distance from Raithal?

The treks to Dayara Bugyal are initiated from Raithal village. During the entire trek, a total distance of 24 km is covered. The distance from Raithal to Gui, which is the initial leg of the journey, comes to a total of 6 km. The route is planned in a way that the trekkers get ample scope and time to get acclimatized to the increasing altitude.

4. How many days is the Dayara Bugyal Trek?

The Dayara Bugyal Trek is done in 4 days. The duration is ideal for the trek to be enjoyed at a comfortable pace with enough rest time, acclimatization, and time to explore the surrounding beauty.

5. Where is the base camp for the Dayara Bugyal trek?

The base camp for the Dayara Bugyal trek is based in Raithal Village. The view of this picturesque village is the starting point, from where final preparations are made and last-minute supplies procured for the trek.

6. How high is Dayara Bugyal, and is it safe for beginners?

The towering altitude of Dayara Bugyal is 3,750 meters above sea level. With a considerable altitude such as this, the difficulty level is relatively moderate and easily attempted by beginners. The gradual ascent and well-marked trails contribute to its suitability for novice trekkers.

7. Where is the last ATM on the route to Raithal?

The last ATM facility is found in Uttarkashi. Trekkers are therefore advised to withdraw sufficient cash here as no further ATM services are available beyond this point on the route to Raithal.

8. What kind of accommodation is included in the Dayara Bugyal Trek package?

Accommodation options range from tents at designated campsites like Gui and Barnal Tal for an outdoor experience to comfortable guesthouses in Raithal for those who want a little more shelter. Additional customized accommodation plans might be added based on the trek package chosen.

9. How do I reach the base camp for the Dayara Bugyal Trek?

The Raithal Village is the starting point that is 182 km away from Dehradun. This scenic drive takes trekkers through the beautiful Uttarakhand scenery, setting the stage for their adventure.

10. What should I pack for the Dayara Bugyal Trek?

The three most vital things to be packed for the Dayara Bugyal Trek will be warm clothing, necessary to fight the cold mountain air; strong and sturdy trekking shoes to move through diverse roadways; and rain gear to prepare against unwanted rainfall. A decent first-aid kit is regarded as essential to treat minor health problems. Other personal needs should also not be forgotten and should be arranged according to demand and necessity for the individual trekker. It is advisable to refer to a complete packing list to make sure everything is there for a comfortable and safe trek.
